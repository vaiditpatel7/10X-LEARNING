HERE ARE SOME IMPORTANTS EXTRACTS FORM THE LATEST NCERT GUIDELINES

Attendance Requirements 2026
Attendance is an important eligibility criterion under the CBSE Board exams 2026 rules. Students must maintain at least 75% attendance throughout the academic year.
Up to 25% absence is allowed only for exceptional reasons such as medical emergencies, bereavement or participation in national-level sports or cultural events.
Proper documentation is mandatory for these exceptions.
Students failing to meet the attendance requirement without valid reasons will not be allowed to appear in board exams.
Internal Assessments Mandatory for Eligibility
Internal assessments are now compulsory according to the CBSE issues Class 10, Class 12 Board exams 2026 New Guidelines to appear in board exams. These assessments are conducted over two years and include:
Periodic tests
Projects and assignments
Classroom participation
Rules for Additional Subjects
The rules of the Additional Subjects are stated clearly in the guidelines:
Class 10 Students: Are allowed a maximum of two more subjects on top of the five compulsory subjects.
All additional subjects must be studied continuously for the full two-year period. Schools without proper CBSE approval, qualified teachers or required facilities cannot offer these subjects. Students from such schools will not be allowed to register for these subjects in board exams.
